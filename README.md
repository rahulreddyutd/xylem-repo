# Project 01 — Smart Leak Detection Sensor Launch

**Category:** New Product Development (NPD)
**Theme:** Full Stage Gate lifecycle — from VOC research to commercialization

---

## Problem Statement

Municipal water utilities in the US lose an estimated 20–30% of treated drinking water to undetected pipe leaks each year. Existing detection methods (acoustic loggers, ground-penetrating radar) are expensive, slow to deploy, and require specialist contractors. Small-to-mid-sized utilities lack the budget and bandwidth to run these programs at scale.

**The opportunity:** a low-cost, self-install IoT acoustic sensor that surfaces real-time leak alerts via a SaaS dashboard — making continuous leak detection accessible to utilities of any size.

---

## My Role

Strategic Product Owner responsible for driving this product from concept through launch, owning the Stage Gate process end to end and coordinating across Engineering, R&D, Sales, Finance, and Marketing.

---

## Process & Methodology

### Stage 1 — Discovery & VOC
- Conducted 10 structured interviews with municipal utility managers across 3 regions
- Used a Jobs-to-be-Done framework to uncover unmet needs beyond stated requirements
- Synthesized findings into a Severity × Frequency matrix to prioritize requirements
- Identified top 3 pain points: (1) delayed leak detection, (2) high contractor cost, (3) no real-time data visibility

### Stage 2 — Requirements & Business Case
- Translated VOC insights into 24 functional and non-functional product requirements in a requirements management tool
- Built a 3-year P&L model with finance: hardware COGS, SaaS ARR, channel margin, and break-even point
- Defined pricing strategy: one-time hardware at $349/unit + $89/month SaaS per site
- Presented Stage Gate 2 review to leadership; received approval with one scope reduction

### Stage 3 — Product Roadmap
Designed a 3-phase roadmap with clear gate criteria:

| Phase | Timeline | Scope |
|-------|----------|-------|
| MVP | Months 1–6 | Acoustic sensor + alert dashboard |
| V2 — Analytics | Months 7–12 | Trend analysis, water loss quantification |
| V3 — Integrations | Months 13–18 | SCADA, GIS, and utility billing API integrations |

### Stage 4 — Development & Beta
- Collaborated with R&D on technical requirement tradeoffs (battery life vs. sensitivity)
- Ran a 6-utility beta program: gathered structured feedback on install experience and alert accuracy
- Managed backlog of 47 issues; prioritized 12 for GA release, deferred 35 to V2

### Stage 5 — Launch & Commercialization
- Built sales enablement kit: product one-pager, ROI calculator, competitive battle card, objection guide
- Coordinated with regional marketing on launch webinar (312 registrants)
- Defined SIOP inputs: 18-month demand forecast by region, supplier lead time risk assessment

# Project 02 — 80/20 SKU Rationalization

**Category:** Portfolio Strategy & Financial Analysis
**Theme:** Pareto-driven portfolio simplification to improve margin and operational efficiency

---

## Problem Statement

A mid-market pump product line had grown organically to 140 active SKUs over 8 years of acquisitions and regional customization. Internal analysis showed that 80% of revenue was concentrated in just 28 SKUs (20%). The remaining 112 SKUs generated disproportionate complexity: higher inventory carrying costs, longer production changeover times, increased demand forecasting error, and confused sales conversations.

**The mandate:** Design and implement an 80/20 rationalization plan that simplifies the portfolio, improves margin, and does not erode key customer relationships.

---

## My Role

Product Manager responsible for leading the full rationalization initiative, owning the data analysis, cross-functional coordination, executive communication, and change management plan.

---

## Process & Methodology

### Phase 1 — Data Collection & Pareto Analysis
- Pulled 3 years of SKU-level data from ERP: revenue, units sold, gross margin, inventory turns, and order frequency
- Segmented SKUs into four tiers using a 2×2 matrix (Revenue × Margin):
  - **A-tier (Invest):** High revenue, high margin — 28 SKUs
  - **B-tier (Maintain):** High revenue, lower margin — 22 SKUs
  - **C-tier (Bundle/Simplify):** Low revenue, acceptable margin — 31 SKUs
  - **D-tier (Exit):** Low revenue, low margin, low frequency — 59 SKUs
- Built a waterfall chart showing cumulative revenue contribution by SKU rank

### Phase 2 — Competitive Landscape Mapping
- Analyzed 3 key competitors (Grundfos, Flowserve, ITT) across: price point, feature set, channel coverage, and regional availability
- Identified whitespace: competitors did not serve the 2–4" municipal pump segment below $1,200 retail — an opportunity preserved in the platform SKU strategy
- Identified threat: one competitor had recently launched a 3-SKU simplified line directly targeting our A-tier

### Phase 3 — Rationalization Recommendations
Presented three scenarios to leadership:

| Scenario | SKUs Exited | Margin Impact | Revenue Risk | Recommendation |
|----------|-------------|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| Conservative | 20 | +1.8pt | Low | Not recommended |
| Moderate | 40 | +4.2pt | Medium | **Selected** |
| Aggressive | 75 | +7.1pt | High | Not recommended |

**Selected scenario details:**
- 40 D-tier SKUs to sunset (12-month wind-down with last-time-buy program)
- 12 C-tier SKUs bundled into 4 platform configurations
- 8 A-tier SKUs designated as strategic platforms with full investment priority
- Customer migration plan for top 14 affected accounts (sales-led, PM-supported)

### Phase 4 — SIOP & Finance Alignment
- Reforecast demand with updated SKU structure; validated with 3 regional sales directors
- Worked with procurement to model inventory liquidation timeline for exited SKUs
- Updated 18-month SIOP with revised production schedule, reducing changeover hours by 22%

### Phase 5 — Executive Presentation
Built a 10-slide deck presented to the VP of Product and CFO:
1. Executive summary & recommendation
2. The complexity problem (data)
3. Pareto analysis
4. Tier segmentation methodology
5. Competitive context
6. Three scenarios with trade-offs
7. Selected scenario deep dive
8. Customer impact & migration plan
9. Financial forecast (3-year)
10. Implementation timeline & owners

---

|-------------|--------|---------|
| SKU Pareto Analysis | Excel | Core data foundation |
| Tier Segmentation Model | Excel | Decision framework |
| Competitor Landscape Matrix | PowerPoint | Context for retention decisions |
| Rationalization Scenarios | PowerPoint | Leadership decision support |
| Customer Migration Playbook | PDF | Sales team execution guide |
| Executive Deck | PowerPoint | VP/CFO approval |

---

## Results & Projected Impact

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| SKUs Exited | 40 (−29% portfolio reduction) |
| Complexity Reduction (by active SKU count) | −71% of tail SKUs |
| Projected Annual Cost Savings | $800K (inventory + production) |
| Gross Margin Improvement | +4.2 percentage points |
| Forecast Accuracy Improvement | +18% (fewer tail SKUs to model) |
| Customer Churn from Rationalization | 0 named accounts lost |

---

## Skills Demonstrated

`80/20 Frameworks` `Pareto Analysis` `Gross Margin Analysis` `SIOP Forecasting` `Competitive Intelligence` `Executive Communication` `Financial Modeling` `Change Management` `Cross-Functional Collaboration` `Portfolio Strategy`

---

## Key Learnings

1. Involving sales early (Phase 1, not Phase 3) prevented pushback that had killed a prior rationalization attempt. Sales became advocates, not opponents.
2. Customer migration plan needed to be built before the exec presentation — leadership wanted to see the plan for protecting relationships before approving exits.
3. The "moderate" scenario consistently outperforms aggressive rationalization in PM practice: preserved optionality while delivering meaningful margin improvement.

# Project 03 — Product Performance Dashboard

**Category:** Data Analysis & Market Intelligence
**Theme:** KPI framework + pricing strategy to enable data-driven product decisions

---

## Problem Statement

Regional sales teams across the North America division were making product and pricing decisions without a consistent data foundation. Product performance reviews happened quarterly at best, using inconsistent metrics pulled from multiple systems. Pricing in some regions was 8–12% below recommended corridors without documented rationale. Market share visibility was limited to annual reports with 6-month lag.

**The goal:** Build a product performance tracking framework — KPI definitions, market analysis, pricing guardrails, and a dashboard — that enables weekly data-driven decisions for product and sales leadership.

---

## My Role

Product Manager responsible for defining the KPI architecture, running the market trend analysis, building the pricing strategy, and producing the dashboard mockup as a reference for the BI team's implementation.

---

## Process & Methodology

### Phase 1 — Stakeholder Alignment on KPIs
- Ran working sessions with 4 stakeholder groups: Product, Sales, Finance, and Operations
- Identified 34 candidate metrics; narrowed to 12 core KPIs through a voting and prioritization exercise
- Assigned metric owners, reporting cadence (weekly/monthly/quarterly), and RAG threshold definitions for each

**Final KPI Framework (12 metrics):**

| Category | KPI | Owner | Cadence |
|----------|-----|-------|---------|
| Revenue | Revenue vs. Plan by Product | Finance | Weekly |
| Revenue | Average Selling Price (ASP) by Region | Finance | Weekly |
| Margin | Gross Margin % by SKU Tier | Finance | Monthly |
| Margin | Discount Rate by Channel | Sales Ops | Weekly |
| Market | Win Rate by Product vs. Competitor | Sales | Monthly |
| Market | Market Share Estimate (3rd party) | PM | Quarterly |
| Customer | NPS by Product Category | PM | Quarterly |
| Customer | Repeat Purchase Rate | Sales Ops | Monthly |
| Pipeline | Pipeline Coverage Ratio | Sales | Weekly |
| Pipeline | Quote-to-Close Rate | Sales | Monthly |
| Operations | Forecast Accuracy (MAPE) | Operations | Monthly |
| Operations | On-Time Delivery % | Operations | Weekly |

### Phase 2 — Market Trend Analysis
Synthesized 6 external sources (EPA, Global Water Intelligence, BlueField Research, IEA, US infrastructure bill tracking, state utility commission filings) to identify 5 macro trends with direct product implications:

1. **Municipal infrastructure spending surge** — $55B allocated through IIJA; utilities prioritizing smart monitoring and leak detection
2. **Water scarcity intensification** — 40 US states projecting water shortfalls by 2030; driving demand for efficiency tech
3. **EPA PFAS regulations** — new treatment requirements creating upgrade cycles for filtration products
4. **Digital utility transformation** — 68% of mid-sized utilities planning AMI or SCADA upgrades in next 3 years
5. **Energy cost pressure** — utility energy costs up 31% since 2021; efficiency and VFD pump products gaining urgency

Each trend was linked to: affected product lines, opportunity size estimate, urgency rating, and recommended action.

### Phase 3 — Pricing Analysis
- Built a Price-to-Value (PtV) map: plotted our portfolio and 3 competitors on axes of perceived value vs. price
- Identified 2 products priced below their value position (underpriced by 8–14%)
- Identified 1 product priced above its perceived value position (at risk in competitive deals)
- Recommended price corridor adjustments by region and channel:

| Product Group | Current ASP | Recommended Floor | Recommended Ceiling | Rationale |
|---------------|------------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Submersible Pumps 2–4" | $880 | $920 | $1,080 | Underpriced vs. value; competitor ceiling is $1,150 |
| VFD Pump Controllers | $2,240 | $2,100 | $2,600 | Value strong; corridor underutilized in direct channel |
| Mechanical Seals | $310 | $290 | $370 | Slight overpricing in distributor channel; adjust floor |

### Phase 4 — Dashboard Design
Produced a detailed dashboard mockup (annotated wireframe + spec document) covering:
- **Header:** Product scorecard summary — 4 metric cards (revenue, margin, win rate, NPS) with vs-prior-period delta
- **Body:** Interactive product/region filter; KPI table with sparklines and RAG indicators; trend line charts for top 3 metrics
- **Alerts panel:** Auto-flagged items where metric is >10% below target for 2+ consecutive weeks
- **Export:** One-click PDF for weekly leadership review

Handed spec to BI team with data source mapping and refresh cadence requirements.

## Skills Demonstrated

`KPI Design` `Data Interpretation` `Market Research` `Trend Analysis` `Pricing Strategy` `Competitive Analysis` `Dashboard Design` `PM Tool Proficiency` `Sales Enablement` `Stakeholder Alignment` `Executive Reporting`

---

## Key Learnings

1. Metric overload is a real risk — initial stakeholder wish list was 34 metrics. Ruthless prioritization to 12 drove adoption; nobody monitors 34 numbers.
2. Pricing recommendations need a clear "why" per region, not just a global corridor — regional sales pushback dropped significantly when the competitive rationale was localized.
3. The dashboard became most valuable not for the data it showed, but for the conversations it forced: teams now discuss the same numbers weekly instead of debating which numbers to use.
